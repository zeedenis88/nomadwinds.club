so, where do we go?

Cumbuco
time: 1 week

One of the oldest and most famous spots, where most kitesurfers start their Brazilian adventure, and so are we. Location close to the airport will allow some flexibility with arrival times. Social vibe and lots of events willallow us to dive deep into Brazilian lifestyle straight from the beginning.

In Cumbuco, you can experience it all: flat-water lagoons just a short downwind or drive away, choppy ocean conditions, and waves.

Day trip
  • Taiba - a spot that has become famous in the world of kitesurfing. A spot where you’ll be kiting alongside some of the best freestyle kitesurfers in the world.

Praia de Macapa
time: 2 weeks

Tiny town, where it's more likely to meet donkeys than humans. Very remote but also breathtaking with beauty. Bring your drone for the epic footage.

It features multiple flat-water lagoons nestled among mangroves at high tide, flat river mouth at low tide and small waves on the ocean side. It’s a perfect playground for progress and one of our favourite spots.

Day trip
  • trip to Parnaiba - boat trip to discover the mangrove forest, dunes, kite alone in the river and eat fresh crabs straight from the river. That's an experience.

Tatajuba
time: 1 week

Probably the most iconic big-air spot in Brazil, Tatajuba is also the windiest location on the coast. It offers a mix of butter-flat lagoons right in front of the house and some of the best right-side kickers in the world in the open ocean. A short drive or downwinder away from other legendary spots, it’s a true kitesurfing paradise.

This small town, nestled in the sand dunes, has no paved roads and is hard to access—but that’s part of its charm. The small but strong community creates a unique atmosphere, and there’s always something going on.

Day trip
  • Downwind Prea-Tatajuba - imagine strong wind, a mixture of flat areas and waves, stunning remote landscape and only you and your friends on the water cruising through
  • Night in Jericoacoara - the ultimate Brazilian party destination surrounded by sand dunes

practical info

Accomodation

In Cumbuco and Macapá, accommodation will be provided in carefully selected local houses with kitchens and common areas suitable for working, all located a convenient distance from the beach. In Tatajuba, due to limited options and the remote nature of the village, we will stay in a pousada that includes breakfast and common areas, but no kitchen.

All properties have been vetted for internet quality, and we’ll also have our own Starlink as a backup. Please note that power outages can occur in Brazil, though they are relatively rare.

The price includes accommodation in twin rooms. Private rooms are available for an additional cost of €800.

Food

Once a week, we’ll gather to cook dinner together, make homemade caipirinhas, and hang out. All ingredients for those meals will be provided. Our communal dinners are a fantastic opportunity to get to know each other and connect.

Otherwise, food and drinks will be your expenses. Dinner prices range from 5€ at local eateries to 25€ at upscale, tourist-oriented restaurants, with options in between. For those on a budget, most of the accommodations include kitchen facilities so you can cook your own meals.

Transportation

At all times, we will have a car available for the group to run errands, do shopping, or go to the ATM. On transfer days, we will provide additional cars with drivers to transport everyone and all the equipment between the spots.

We will also share contacts for affordable taxis, buggies, and other services to help avoid 'gringo prices' when organizing additional trips or activities.

The airport transfers are not included but we can help you arrange the accredited transfer for Cumbuco (extra cost of 25€). Uber is an option but officially they are not allowed to transport quivers so many drivers decline.
