Where do we go?

Dakhla is a small peninsula that separates a stunning turquoise lagoon from the ocean, surrounded by golden sand dunes. It’s a unique location where these two striking landscapes meet, creating an ideal environment for kitesurfing.

The lagoon is well-suited for kitesurfers of all levels. In July, the wind is at its best, blowing 20-30 knots every day - so be sure to pack your smaller kites. The water at 20-22 degrees allows to use a short wetsuit or a light full wetsuit depending on your preference.

The main kite spot is located just a few meters from the accommodation, making it easy to kite whenever it fits your schedule. Beyond this, the area offers a variety of conditions, that we will explore during day trips.

For a change of scenery, the town of Dakhla is just 30 minutes away, where you we will immerse ourselves in Arabic culture, visiting the markets and local restaurants.

Accomodation

We will stay in North Lagoon - a highly rated, small and intimate camp located in the northern corner of the lagoon, just a few steps from the water. Surrounded by peaceful desert landscape, the camp offers everything you need on-site.

Accommodation will be provided in comfortable twin bungalows. Each bungalow includes a private bathroom and two separate beds (a double bed is available on request), offering breathtaking views of the lagoon.

Outside the rooms, there are several common areas available for work and relaxation. Lockers for kitesurfing equipment are included in the price, ensuring convenience for all participants.

Meals will be served in the camp’s restaurant. The full board includes three buffet-style meals per day, featuring Moroccan dishes prepared with fresh, local ingredients.

The cool stuff

During the weekends we will explore everything Dakhla has to offer off-the-beaten path. The program includes three day trips to the hidden gems of the lagoon - from-butter flat offshore waters to downwind routes leading to remote, untouched locations. We’ll introduce new spots progressively, starting with the easiest and advancing as everyone gains confidence and skill. For those interested in additional exploration, we’ll provide guidance and contacts to help you organize extra trips independently.

The trip also includes two in-water coaching sessions, complemented by evening video analysis to accelerate your progress. We’ll work with each participant to identify individual goals and provide tailored guidance to help you achieve them, with plenty of time to practice between coaching sessions.

We offer additional coaching for advanced riders and lessons or supervision for beginners, all led by certified IKO instructors, available at an extra cost.

About Us

Aleksandra launched Nomad Winds with a trip to Brazil in October 2024. An adventurer and digital nomad, she has been working remotely from windy places since 2021. She has traveled to over 40 countries and lived in 10 across four continents. Apart from chasing the wind and aiming for the next WOO PR (13,9 m), Aleksandra sometimes works as a design researcher and strategist. Lately, she’s been finding joy in designing travel experiences.

Denis, a remote software engineer born and raised in the French Alps, swapped his favorite pair of skis for a kite quiver at the end of 2021 in Cape Town. After surviving learning at one of the world’s most challenging spots, he solidified his passion for the sport.

Aleksandra and Denis reconnected in Tarifa in 2024 and have been inseparable since, chasing the wind together across six countries on three continents. Now they are joining forces at Nomad Winds to explore new destinations and share it with others.

Nomad Winds delivers community-driven co-living and co-working expeditions. It was born from the desire to be surrounded with like-minded people even in remote locations, and to foster a sense of community in a lifestyle that can sometimes feel isolating.
